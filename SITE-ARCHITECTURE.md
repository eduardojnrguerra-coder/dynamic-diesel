# Dyna-Mic Diesel Site Architecture

Purpose: define the scalable website structure before further SEO expansion. This document does not create new pages by itself.

## Primary Positioning

Dyna-Mic Diesel should be positioned first as a complete commercial truck repair, servicing, maintenance and fleet support workshop based in Anderbolt, Boksburg.

Brand expertise supports that positioning:

- Specialist experience: Volvo, Scania, Mercedes-Benz and Mercedes Actros.
- Additional confirmed brands serviced and repaired: MAN, DAF, Iveco, Hino, Isuzu, UD Trucks, FAW, FUSO and Renault Trucks.
- Additional brands must be described as serviced, maintained and repaired, not as specialist brands unless the business confirms that wording later.

Every truck brought into Dyna-Mic Diesel receives a complimentary truck health inspection intended to help identify potential maintenance concerns and developing problems. Do not describe this as a guarantee.

## Primary Navigation

Keep the main header navigation as:

- Home
- Services
- About Us
- Contact Us

Do not add every service, truck brand, industry or advice category to the header.

## Duplicate Search Intent Risks

- `/fleet-maintenance` vs `/fleet-maintenance-boksburg`
- `/services/diesel-engine-diagnostics` vs `/diesel-diagnostics-boksburg`
- `/services/engine-dyno-testing` vs `/engine-dyno-testing-boksburg`
- `/services/diesel-pumps-and-injectors` vs `/diesel-pumps-and-injectors-boksburg`
- `/services/truck-services-maintenance-and-repairs` vs `/truck-servicing-boksburg`
- Dynamic `/brands/*` pages vs top-level brand pages.
- `/blog` vs any future `/truck-advice` hub.

Recommended approach: keep existing indexed URLs live, select one primary page for each search intent, and point internal links toward the primary page. Use redirects only when a page is truly redundant and migration risk has been assessed.

## Recommended Implementation Order

1. Finalise Services hub and make top-level service pages the primary internal-link targets.
2. Create `/truck-brands` hub.
3. Create additional confirmed brand pages one brand at a time, starting with MAN, Hino, Isuzu and DAF if demand is expected.
4. Create `/fleet-services` hub and fleet enquiry page.
5. Create fleet inspection and preventative fleet maintenance pages.
6. Create `/industries` hub before individual industry pages.
7. Create `/truck-advice` hub and category structure.
8. Gradually publish article content based on search demand and internal-link needs.
9. Review sitemap, redirects, canonicals and internal links after every batch.
